कार्तिक मास की शुक्ल पक्ष की नवमी को अयोध्या के चौदहकोस परिवृत्त से परिक्रमा की जाती है। इस परिक्रमा को करने के लिए भक्त अयोध्या आते हैं और स्थानीय लोग नवमी का इंतजार करते हैं। तिथि के आने पर सरयू नदी में स्नान करके परिक्रमा मार्ग को शीशे से स्पर्श करके भगवान का ध्यान करते हुए परिक्रमा आरंभ करते हैं। परिक्रमा के दौरान, नंगे पैरों पर चलते हुए भजन आदि करते हुए परिक्रमा का प्रारंभ स्थल पर पहुँचकर, वे अयोध्या के मंदिरों का दर्शन करके अपने गृह लौटते हैं।

आयोध्या में मुख्य रूप से 3 प्रकार की परिक्रमाएँ होती हैं: पहली 84 कोसी, दूसरी 14 कोसी, और तीसरी 5 कोसी की। 1 कोस में 3 किलोमीटर होते हैं। आयोध्या की सीमा तीन भागों में बाँटी है: 84 कोसी में अवध क्षेत्र, 14 कोसी में आयोध्या नगर, और 5 कोसी में आयोध्या क्षेत्र है। इसलिए, तीन प्रकार की परिक्रमा होती है। 84 कोसी परिक्रमा में साधू-संत भाग लेते हैं, जबकि 14 कोसी और 5 कोसी परिक्रमा में आम लोग शामिल होते हैं।

परिक्रमा का मुख्य उद्देश्य है कि हिन्दू धर्म के अनुसार जीवात्मा 84 लाख योनियों में भ्रमण करती है, और इस प्रक्रिया के दौरान जन्म जन्मांतर में किए गए पापों का नाश होता है। कहा जाता है कि परिक्रमा करते समय पाप नष्ट होते हैं।

14 कोसी परिक्रमा, जिसे कार्तिक परिक्रमा भी कहा जाता है, वर्ष में एक बार होती है, और इसके दौरान भगवान विष्णु का देवोथान (जागना) होता है, जिससे कार्यों में कोई क्षरण नहीं होता और जो प्रतिभागी के लिए आनंददायक होता है। अगर आप मन से इसमें भाग लें, तो आपको इसके फल की प्राप्ति होती है।

Introduction

Ayodhya, a city steeped in Hindu mythology and divine significance, holds a special place in the hearts of millions of devotees. The annual pilgrimage of 14 Kosi Parikrama is a cherished tradition that connects worshippers with their faith and offers them a chance to embark on a spiritual odyssey like no other.

Ayodhya: The Abode of Lord Ram

Ayodhya, nestled on the banks of the sacred Sarayu River, is hailed as the birthplace of Lord Ram, the seventh incarnation of Lord Vishnu. This ancient city resonates with historical and cultural importance, casting a spellbinding aura on visitors and believers alike. With its rich mythology and numerous sacred sites, Ayodhya is an embodiment of divine grace.

Mythological stories depicting Lord Ram’s illustrious life are deeply intertwined with Ayodhya’s heritage. It is in this very city that Lord Ram spent his childhood, ruling over his kingdom, and ultimately departing for Vaikuntha after his mortal journey. The epic Ramayana narrates numerous extraordinary events that transpired in Ayodhya, leaving behind a legacy steeped in spirituality.

Understanding the Concept of Parikrama

Parikrama, a Sanskrit word meaning “circumambulation,” signifies a spiritual journey undertaken by devotees. It involves walking around a sacred site, temple, or any other revered object in a clockwise direction to show devotion and seek blessings. Parikrama is an integral part of Hindu religious practices, believed to purify the soul and bring worshippers closer to the divine.

The circumference covered during a parikrama can vary, ranging from a few steps to several kilometers. In the case of the 14 Kosi Parikrama in Ayodhya, devotees embark on a journey spanning approximately 48 kilometers, immersing themselves in the path of spirituality and self-discovery.

The Mystical Legend of 14 Kosi Parikrama

The origins and historical background of the 14 Kosi Parikrama are shrouded in mystical allure. Legend has it that the tradition dates back to ancient times when the revered sage Vasishtha, along with his wife Arundhati, performed this sacred circumambulation to seek the blessings of Lord Ram. Since then, this 14 Kosi Parikrama has been a sacred pilgrimage route followed by ardent devotees.

Intriguing tales and legends associated with the parikrama add to its mystique. It is believed that following the 14 Kosi Parikrama with unwavering devotion fulfills wishes and brings about spiritual enlightenment. Many devotees share their personal anecdotes, recounting miraculous experiences and divine encounters during this transformative pilgrimage.

Preparing for the Spiritual Odyssey

Embarking on the 14 Kosi Parikrama requires adequate preparation to ensure a smooth and safe spiritual journey. Here are some essential preparations and requirements to consider:

  • Physical Fitness: The arduous trek demands a certain level of physical fitness. It is advisable to engage in regular exercise, such as walking or jogging, to build stamina and endurance before undertaking the parikrama.
  • Proper Attire: Dressing modestly and comfortably is crucial for pilgrims. Lightweight clothing that allows freedom of movement is recommended, along with sturdy walking shoes and a hat to protect from the sun.
  • Water and Snacks: Carrying an ample supply of drinking water and energy-boosting snacks is essential to stay hydrated and energized throughout the journey.
  • Safety Measures: It is crucial to carry a basic first aid kit, sunscreen, insect repellent, and any necessary medications. Also, staying alert and following the instructions of the organizers ensures a safe parikrama experience.

Day 1: The Journey Begins

The first day of the 14 Kosi Parikrama marks the commencement of the sacred trek. Pilgrims gather at the designated starting point, immerse themselves in prayers, and set out with hearts filled with devotion. The route for the first leg of the parikrama takes devotees through enchanting lanes, ancient temples, and significant landmarks.

As the pilgrims wind their way through the initial stretch, they encounter revered sites such as Nageshwarnath Temple, Mani Parvat, and Ram Janmabhoomi, where Lord Ram is believed to have taken birth. The air is filled with the fragrance of incense, and the devotees are enveloped in a serene aura that adds to the spiritual fervor of the journey.

Day 2: Continuing the Sacred Trek

The 14 Kosi Parikrama, a continuous spiritual expedition, carries on into the second day. On this day, participants traverse through a path that intertwines with legends and divine stories. As the devotees walk along the holy Sarayu River, they come across sacred spots like Guptar Ghat, where Lord Ram is believed to have disappeared, and Sita Kund, which holds mythical significance.

The natural beauty that surrounds the parikrama path adds an ethereal touch to the experience. With lush green landscapes, blooming flowers, and the melodious chirping of birds, pilgrims find solace and tranquility amidst mother nature’s embrace.

Day 3: Immersed in Devotion

The third day of the parikrama journey dawns upon the devotees, marking another day of intense devotion and profound experiences. This leg of the pilgrimage takes the participants through ancient temples that bear testimony to Ayodhya’s spiritual heritage.

Devotees visit Hanuman Garhi, a revered temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman, known for his unwavering devotion to Lord Ram. The atmosphere is charged with spiritual energy as pilgrims seek blessings from this mighty deity, reinforcing their determination and faith.

Beyond the temples, the parikrama route leads worshippers to holy places like Chakra Tirth, where it is believed that Lord Ram performed rituals for the welfare of mankind. These revered sites serve as reminders of the rich history and profound spirituality that permeate Ayodhya’s every corner.

Day 4: Reaching the Culminating Point

As the final day of the 14 Kosi Parikrama approaches, anticipation and excitement grip the devotees. The spiritual odyssey nears its culmination, and every step taken brings them closer to their ultimate goal. On this day, pilgrims traverse the remaining portion of the route, journeying through sacred sites that hold immense significance.

One of the highlights of the final leg of the parikrama is the visit to Kanak Bhawan, a temple dedicated to Lord Ram and his beloved consort, Goddess Sita. Devotees experience a sense of fulfillment and immense joy as they offer their prayers in this sacred abode of the divine couple.

Celebrations and Rituals at the End of the Parikrama

Upon completing the 14 Kosi Parikrama, the holy journey concludes with elaborate religious ceremonies and vibrant celebrations. Devotees gather to express their gratitude, perform Aarti (a ritual of waving lighted lamps), and partake in communal feasts known as Prasad. The atmosphere pulsates with devotion and a shared sense of accomplishment.

Witnessing the fervor and devotion of the participants is truly awe-inspiring. Their unwavering faith and dedication serve as a testament to the power of spiritual connection and the transformative impact of the 14 Kosi Parikrama.

Exploring the Mesmerizing Beauty of Ayodhya

Beyond the spiritual aspects, Ayodhya offers a wealth of mesmerizing beauty and serene locales waiting to be discovered. Visitors can immerse themselves in the tranquility of places like Ram Ki Paidi, a sacred bathing ghat on the Sarayu River, or explore the stunning gardens of Gulab Bari.

Engaging with the locals provides an opportunity for a deeper cultural experience. Conversations with Ayodhya residents reveal anecdotes, folklore, and myriad tales surrounding the city’s rich heritage. From savoring traditional delicacies to witnessing local artisans creating intricate handicrafts, Ayodhya rewards visitors with an unforgettable blend of spirituality and cultural immersion.

Significance of Ayodhya in Contemporary India

Ayodhya continues to hold immense significance in modern-day Hinduism. With the construction of the magnificent Ram temple at the disputed Ram Janmabhoomi site, Ayodhya has become a focal point of religious unity and devotion for Hindus across the world. The city’s spiritual legacy has profoundly impacted Indian society, bridging the gap between generations and reviving the fervor of faith.

The pilgrimage of the 14 Kosi Parikrama serves as a catalyst in keeping Ayodhya’s significance alive. It acts as a reminder of the ancient traditions and spiritual heritage that continue to shape the lives of millions.

FAQs: Clearing Doubts and Queries

Here are some of the frequently asked questions regarding the 14 Kosi Parikrama:

  • Where can I find accommodation during the parikrama?
    • Ayodhya offers a range of lodging options to suit different budgets. From hotels to guesthouses and dharamshalas (pilgrim rest houses), there are ample choices available for pilgrims.
  • Is the parikrama safe for solo travelers?
    • The parikrama is a well-organized event with proper security measures in place. However, it is still advisable for solo travelers to exercise caution, travel in groups if possible, and stay informed about any relevant safety guidelines.
  • Are there any restrictions during the parikrama?
    • Certain areas along the parikrama route may have specific rules or guidelines to maintain sanctity and security. It is essential to respect these restrictions to ensure a harmonious pilgrimage experience.

Conclusion: A Spiritual Sojourn to Remember

The 14 Kosi Parikrama in Ayodhya offers a transformative journey that takes devotees through a path of spirituality, history, and natural beauty. From the captivating tales of Lord Ram and Ayodhya’s rich heritage to the rigorous trek through sacred sites, this spiritual odyssey promises an experience like no other.

As the devotees conclude their parikrama, their hearts are filled with a sense of fulfillment and tranquility. The pilgrimage leaves an indelible mark on their souls, reminding them of the power of faith and the eternal bond between humanity and the divine.

Embarking on the 14 Kosi Parikrama is an opportunity to connect with the spiritual essence of Ayodhya and immerse oneself in a sacred sojourn that illuminates the path to self-discovery and enlightenment. Take that first step and allow the divine aura of Ayodhya to envelop your being, for an unforgettable spiritual odyssey awaits.
