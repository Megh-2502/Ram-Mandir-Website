Places to Visit

नागेश्वर नाथ मंदिर

The temple of Nageshwarnath is said to have been established by Kush, the son of Rama. Kush erected this temple for the Nag-kanya who fell in love with him.The present temple was built in 1750 by the minister of Safdar Jung, named Naval Rai. The festival of Shivratri is celebrated here in a big way

देव काली मंदिर

This temple is associated with several tales from the Ramayana and is situated near Naya Ghat. As per mythology, Mother Sita after her wedding with Lord Rama arrived in Ayodhya with an idol of goddess Girija Devi. King Dashrath got a beautiful temple constructed for the idol and Mother Sita used to worship the Devi in the temple. An impressive statue of Goddess Devkali, the local deity, is present now

राम की पैड़ी

Ram Ki Paidi Category Religious Ram ki Paidi is a series of ghats on the bank of River Saryu. The river front brings forth an outstanding Landscape especially in floodlit night. These serve as platforms to devotees who it is said, come to wash away their sins by taking a dip in the river

कनक भवन

Kanak Bhawan is one of the finest and famous temples in Ayodhya.It is believed that this Bhawan was gifted to Devi Sita by Kaikei immediately after her marriage to Lord Ram. This is private palace of Devi Sita and Lord Rama. Later it was rebuilt/renovated by Vrish Bhanu Kunwari .The main idols installed in the garbhgrih (sanctum), are of Lord Ram and Devi Sit

हनुमान गढ़ी

Hanuman Garhi is 1 km from Ayodhya railway station. Vikramaditya got a temple constructed which later on came to be known as Hanuman Garhi. It is believed that Lord Hanuman (Pavan Putra) lived here guard Ayodhya. You will find here a beautiful idol of Bal (young) sitting on lap of Maa Anjani at the main temple.

बिरला मंदिर

Shri Ram Janaki Birla Temple is a newly made temple. It is located opposite to the Ayodhya Bus stop on the way of Ayodhya-Faizabad. This temple is dedicated to Lord Ram and Devi Sita
